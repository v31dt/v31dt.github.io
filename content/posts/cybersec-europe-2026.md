Cybersec Europe 2026 took place at Brussels Expo on 20 and 21 May 2026. It is one of the largest cybersecurity events in the Benelux, with a mix of vendors, talks, technical sessions, demos, and networking opportunities. I attended it with friends from my cybersecurity circle: Artūrs Jakovļevs, Rostyslav Fedorov, Fedor Vinogradov, and Vladyslav Rovnyi.

The target audience was broad but still very relevant for me: security professionals, students, vendors, CISOs, engineers, and people working around governance, cloud, identity, data protection, and threat intelligence. That mix made the event useful because it did not feel limited to one narrow area of security. There were technical topics, but also discussions about strategy, policy, resilience, and how organisations should think about security at scale.

The session that stayed with me most was **"A Holistic Approach to Cyber Threats"** by Jo De Muynck from ENISA. The main value for me was hearing a European agency perspective on the changing threat landscape, especially around AI. In class and in my own projects, I often look at AI from a technical angle: how it can help with recon, automation, malware analysis, or security tooling. This talk pushed me to think more about the wider picture. AI is not only a tool for defenders or researchers. It also changes how threat actors scale, adapt, and combine attacks.

Professionally, the event was also useful because it made the cybersecurity field feel less abstract. Talking to people, walking past company booths, and seeing what vendors are building helped me connect my own interests with the industry. My current focus is offensive security, mobile testing, malware analysis, and automation, but events like this show how those skills fit into a bigger ecosystem.

I would attend Cybersec Europe again. The talks were useful, the networking was strong, and the event gave a good overview of what companies and public organisations currently care about. I would also recommend it to other cybersecurity students, especially if they want to understand what exists beyond coursework and CTF-style challenges.
